## Introduction

Tokyo is a city of contrasts where ancient temples stand alongside futuristic skyscrapers. This guide will help you experience the best of Tokyo in 5 days, from traditional neighborhoods to modern attractions.

## Day 1: Shibuya & Harajuku

### Morning: Meiji Shrine
Start your Tokyo adventure at this peaceful Shinto shrine surrounded by forest in the heart of the city.

### Afternoon: Harajuku
Explore Takeshita Street for quirky fashion and street food. Don't miss the famous crepes!

### Evening: Shibuya Crossing
Experience the world's busiest pedestrian crossing and explore Shibuya's shopping and dining scene.

**Where to Eat:**
- Ichiran Ramen for solo dining booths
- Genki Sushi for conveyor belt sushi
- Shibuya Sky for sunset views and cocktails

## Day 2: Asakusa & Ueno

### Morning: Senso-ji Temple
Visit Tokyo's oldest temple and browse Nakamise Shopping Street for traditional souvenirs.

### Afternoon: Ueno Park
Explore museums, temples, and beautiful gardens in this cultural hub.

### Evening: Ameyoko Market
Shop for snacks, clothes, and cosmetics at this bustling market street.

**Where to Eat:**
- Sometaro for okonomiyaki
- Ippudo for ramen
- Izakaya under the train tracks

## Day 3: Tsukiji & Ginza

### Morning: Tsukiji Outer Market
Sample fresh seafood and street food at this famous market.

### Afternoon: Ginza Shopping
Window shop luxury brands and visit department store food halls.

### Evening: Tokyo Tower
See the city lights from this iconic landmark.

**Where to Eat:**
- Sushi Dai for fresh sushi
- Ginza Kagari for chicken ramen
- Tempura Kondo for high-end tempura

## Day 4: Shinjuku & Odaiba

### Morning: Shinjuku Gyoen
Relax in this beautiful garden, especially stunning during cherry blossom season.

### Afternoon: Odaiba
Visit this futuristic island with shopping malls, teamLab Borderless, and waterfront views.

### Evening: Shinjuku Nightlife
Explore Golden Gai's tiny bars or Omoide Yokocho's yakitori alleys.

**Where to Eat:**
- Fuunji for tsukemen
- Omoide Yokocho for yakitori
- Robot Restaurant for a unique show

## Day 5: Day Trip Options

### Option 1: Nikko
Visit ornate shrines and natural beauty (2 hours from Tokyo).

### Option 2: Kamakura
See the Great Buddha and explore temples by the sea (1 hour from Tokyo).

### Option 3: Mount Fuji
View Japan's iconic mountain from Hakone or Kawaguchiko.

## Practical Tips

### Transportation
- Get a Suica or Pasmo card for easy train travel
- Download Google Maps for navigation
- Last trains run around midnight

### Money
- Many places are cash-only
- ATMs at 7-Eleven accept foreign cards

### Etiquette
- Don't eat while walking
- Queue properly and don't push
- Remove shoes when required
- Speak quietly on trains

### Best Time to Visit
- Spring (March-May): Cherry blossoms
- Fall (September-November): Autumn colors
- Avoid Golden Week (late April-early May)

## Where to Stay

### Budget: Asakusa
Traditional neighborhood with hostels and budget hotels.

### Mid-Range: Shinjuku
Central location with excellent transport connections.

### Luxury: Ginza
High-end shopping district with luxury hotels.

## Must-Try Foods

1. **Ramen** - Rich noodle soup
2. **Sushi** - Fresh from Tsukiji
3. **Tempura** - Lightly battered and fried
4. **Okonomiyaki** - Savory pancake
5. **Yakitori** - Grilled chicken skewers
6. **Tonkatsu** - Breaded pork cutlet
7. **Matcha desserts** - Green tea everything
8. **Convenience store food** - Better than you'd expect!

## Hidden Gems

### Yanaka
Old Tokyo neighborhood that survived WWII bombing.

### Kagurazaka
French-influenced area with cobblestone streets.

### Shimokitazawa
Hipster neighborhood with vintage shops and cafes.

## Conclusion

Tokyo offers endless discoveries, from ancient traditions to cutting-edge technology. This itinerary covers the highlights, but don't be afraid to wander and find your own favorite spots. The city rewards curiosity and exploration.

**Pro Tip:** Learn basic Japanese phrases - locals appreciate the effort, and it enhances your experience!
